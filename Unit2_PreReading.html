UNIT 2 · PRE-READING

Reclaim Your Creative Confidence

Tom Kelley & David KelleyHarvard Business Review · 2012
Key framework. The 4 Fears that Block Creativity

Key Takeaways

  1. Creative confidence is not a talent — it is a rebuilt muscle.
  2. Four fears do the blocking: fear of the messy unknown, fear of judgement, fear of the first step, fear of losing control.
  3. Each fear responds to a specific intervention — small successes for the messy unknown, separation from ego for judgement, bias to action for the first step, and compartmentalisation for control.
  4. Executives are as creatively anxious as children — we simply learn to hide it.
  5. Small creative acts compound — the most reliable path to creative confidence is volume, not quality.

Discussion Questions

  1. Which of the 4 fears shapes your own creative behaviour most? What pattern does it produce?
  2. How would your ideation sessions change if you designed them to defeat a specific fear?
  3. What 'small creative act' could you commit to daily for 30 days?
Kelley, T., & Kelley, D. (2012). Reclaim Your Creative Confidence. Harvard Business Review, 90(12), 115–118.

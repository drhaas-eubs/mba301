UNIT 2 · CASE STUDY

Airbnb — From Air Mattresses to Ideas

2007–2011Ideation

Background

When Brian Chesky and Joe Gebbia couldn't pay rent on their San Francisco apartment in 2007, they rented out air mattresses to conference attendees. That desperate improvisation became Airbnb. What makes the case a canonical ideation story is not the first idea but the relentless sequence of reframes — from 'conference couchsurfing' to 'a platform for anyone to rent their home' to 'belong anywhere'.

Key Facts

  • First prototype: three air mattresses · DIYConf 2007
  • First site: airbedandbreakfast.com — handled only conference attendees
  • Pivot moment: 2009 — decided to include any listing, anywhere
  • Re-brand: 2011 — 'Airbnb' + 'belong anywhere' narrative anchor
  • Method: relentless worst-possible-ideas, reframing, and customer immersion (Chesky flew to NYC to photograph listings himself)

Discussion Questions

  1. At which stage did 'worst possible ideas' thinking appear to drive the breakthrough?
  2. What specific divergence method would you expect the team to have used for the belongings → 'belong anywhere' reframe?
  3. How does this case illustrate convergence tools (NUF, dot voting) being used in small teams?
  4. What constraints — legal, financial, narrative — forced ideation discipline here?

References

  • Chesky, B. (2012). Seven Rejections. Medium essay.

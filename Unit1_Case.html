UNIT 1 · CASE STUDY

IDEO Shopping Cart

ABC Nightline · 1999Design Thinking as Mindset

Background

In 1999 ABC's Nightline commissioned IDEO to redesign a supermarket shopping cart in five days. The resulting broadcast became the defining public demonstration of design thinking — and of IDEO's collaborative, rapid, observation-driven method. It remains the single most-taught DT case.

Key Facts

  • Team: multidisciplinary — engineers, biologists, linguists, psychologists
  • Method: observe → brainstorm → prototype → iterate
  • Ground rules: 'Defer judgment. One conversation at a time. Stay focused. Encourage wild ideas. Build on the ideas of others.'
  • Outcome: a modular cart with detachable baskets, angled wheels, and child-safety features — delivered in 5 days
  • Legacy: the broadcast seeded 'design thinking' as a corporate term

Discussion Questions

  1. Why did IDEO observe shoppers in stores instead of consulting retail experts?
  2. Which of the five ground rules is hardest to enforce in real teams, and why?
  3. Where would DVF tensions have been sharpest in this project?
  4. What does this case illustrate about the difference between 'design' and 'design thinking'?

References

  • Kelley, T. (2001). The Art of Innovation. Currency/Doubleday.
  • ABC Nightline (1999). The Deep Dive. Broadcast 13 July.

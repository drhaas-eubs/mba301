UNIT 5 · PRE-READING

Know Your Customers' Jobs to Be Done

Clayton M. Christensen et al.Harvard Business Review · 2016
Key framework. The JTBD Canvas & 4 Forces of the Switch Interview

Key Takeaways

  1. Customers don't buy products, they hire them — to make progress in specific circumstances.
  2. The functional job is only the surface — emotional and social jobs predict switching behaviour more strongly.
  3. The 4 Forces (Push of current · Pull of new · Anxiety · Habit) determine whether a switch happens at all.
  4. Switch interviews — not focus groups — surface the actual forces involved, because they reconstruct a real decision.
  5. JTBD is not a replacement for personas or segmentation — it complements them with an explanation of why anyone switches.

Discussion Questions

  1. Pick a product you recently switched to. Apply the 4 Forces to your switch. Which force was dominant?
  2. What functional / emotional / social jobs is your own product (or your employer's) hired for today?
  3. How would your roadmap change if you optimised for the emotional job rather than the functional one?
Christensen, C.M., Hall, T., Dillon, K., & Duncan, D.S. (2016). Know Your Customers' 'Jobs to Be Done'. Harvard Business Review, 94(9), 54–62.

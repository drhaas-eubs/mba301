Airbnb's First Deck

2008 · The 10 slides that raised a companyThe Art of Pitching

Background

Airbnb's original 2008 pitch deck has been dissected more than almost any other — because its structure is a near-perfect instantiation of what Kawasaki, Sequoia, and StoryBrand prescribe. Ten slides. One idea per slide. A clear StoryBrand arc from 'people need accommodation for conferences' to 'belong anywhere'. The deck was used to raise Airbnb's $600K seed round from Y Combinator and Sequoia.

Key Facts

  • Context: 2008 seed round — raised $600K from Y Combinator + Sequoia
  • Format: exactly 10 slides — Problem, Solution, Market Validation, Market Size, Product, Business Model, Adoption Strategy, Competition, Team, Press
  • Longest slide: 4 bullet points. Most: 1–2
  • Narrative: follows StoryBrand arc — Hero (traveller), Problem (hotels), Guide (Airbnb), Plan (easy booking), Call (book now), Failure avoided, Success
  • Legacy: the deck is now distributed as a free template by Airbnb's own investors

Discussion Questions

  1. Walk through the deck and map each slide to one of Miller's 7 StoryBrand elements. Where is the 'Guide' made most explicit?
  2. Where does the deck demonstrate Kawasaki's 10/20/30 discipline? Where does it deviate, and why?
  3. Which of the 10 slides does the weakest job by modern investor-deck standards? How would you reinforce it?
  4. Apply Bostelaar's Distill-Decompose-Prioritise. What is the one sentence that survives after you distill all 10 slides?

References

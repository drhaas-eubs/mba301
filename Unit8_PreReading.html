Clarify Your Core Message

Kurt BostelaarLeverlo · 2024
Key framework. Distill · Decompose · Prioritise

Key Takeaways

  1. A core message is not your tagline — it is the single sentence your audience can repeat 24 hours later.
  2. Distill: strip the idea to 'we help X to Y by Z'. If it doesn't fit, it's not distilled.
  3. Decompose: break the message into its supporting beats — each beat must be recognisable as a vehicle (metaphor, story, data, demo, quote).
  4. Prioritise: rank the beats by audience relevance — the one you'd keep if you only had 30 seconds must be at the top.
  5. The Message Strategy Spectrum (rational → emotional → ethical) determines which vehicle fits which audience.

Discussion Questions

  1. Apply Distill to your current project. What is the one sentence?
  2. List the 3 most-used 'vehicles' in your work. Which one do you over-use?
  3. For your next pitch, which point on the rational-emotional-ethical spectrum is appropriate? Why?

UNIT 9 · ACTIVITY

Systems Mapping Lab · Capstone

3 × 120 min + capstone deliverySystems Thinking

Objective

Map a real wicked problem using the Iceberg, Meadows' 12 Leverage Points, and at least one systems archetype — then propose and justify one intervention.

Materials

  • Iceberg Canvas (A1)
  • Causal Loop Diagram toolkit
  • Meadows' 12 Points handout
  • Archetypes reference card
  • Theory U reflection guide

Steps

Step 1 · Session 1 · 20 min
Pick a Wicked Problem

Choose one: urban housing · fast fashion · youth mental health · water stress · misinformation. It must cross multiple domains.

Step 2 · Session 1 · 45 min
Iceberg Analysis

Fill all 4 levels: Events, Patterns, Structures, Mental Models. Cite one data source per level.

Step 3 · Session 1 · 45 min
Causal Loop Diagram

Identify 2 reinforcing and 2 balancing loops. Mark delays.

Step 4 · Session 2 · 30 min
Archetype Identification

Does your system match a classic archetype? If so, which variables does the archetype predict will resist intervention?

Step 5 · Session 2 · 45 min
Leverage Point Analysis

For each of Meadows' 12 points, ask: is an intervention available here? Which points are ignored by current policy?

Step 6 · Session 2 · 45 min
Intervention Design

Design one intervention at the highest-leverage point you can defend. Use Theory U — what must be sensed, presenced, and realised?

Step 7 · Session 3
Capstone Delivery

15-minute presentation: Iceberg · CLD · Archetype · Chosen leverage point · Intervention design. 10-minute Q&A. Peer grading included.

Deliverable. Systems Map (PDF, A1 landscape) + 2000-word intervention brief + 15-minute recorded presentation. Combined = capstone deliverable.

Rubric

Knowledge & Understanding 20% · Application 30% · Critical Thinking 25% · Communication 15% · AI Use 10%.

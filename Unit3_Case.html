UNIT 3 · CASE STUDY

Nespresso — Redesigning Coffee

Nestlé · 1986–2020Strategy Design

Background

When Nespresso launched in 1986 it failed so completely that Nestlé considered shutting the project. A decade of strategic redesign — rethinking the customer (from offices to affluent individuals), the channel (boutiques, not supermarkets), and the business model (razor/blade capsules) — turned it into one of the most profitable units in Nestlé's portfolio by 2020. It is the canonical case of strategy design reshaping a product into a category.

Key Facts

  • Initial positioning: office espresso machines — failed
  • Re-positioning (1990s): affluent individual consumers
  • Distribution: Nespresso boutiques, direct-to-consumer club
  • Model: razor/blade (low-margin machines, high-margin capsules)
  • Moat: patented capsule system + brand (Clooney · 'What else?')
  • Legacy: patent expiry 2012 → fragmented capsule competitors, but boutique + club retained

Discussion Questions

  1. Apply Porter's 5 Forces to Nespresso in 2005 vs 2015. What changed?
  2. Draw the Strategy Canvas for Nespresso vs traditional ground-coffee brands. Which ERRC moves stand out?
  3. Where is the Business Model Canvas strongest? Where is the DVF evidence thinnest today?
  4. What Blue Ocean move would you propose now that the capsule patent has expired?

References

  • Kim, W.C. & Mauborgne, R. (2005). Blue Ocean Strategy. HBS Press.
  • Matzler, K. et al. (2013). 'The crusade of corporate giants against startups' — on capsule wars.

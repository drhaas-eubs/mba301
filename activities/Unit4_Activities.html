MBA301 · Unit 4 · Activity

Uncertainty Challenge

Entrepreneurial Thinking

© 2026 Dr. H. Haas · EU Business School

FormatUncertainty Challenge
Duration90 minutes
GroupingTeams of 4

Brief

Bird-in-hand, affordable loss and lemonade cards played against a live constraint.

Learning Objectives

  • Distinguish risk (measurable) from uncertainty (unknowable) in a live business scenario.
  • Apply Sarasvathy's five principles of effectuation as discrete, practisable moves.
  • Build a first-24-hours action plan that starts from means, not ends.

Materials

  • Effectuation card deck (5 principles × multiple prompts)
  • Constraint cards (black — market shocks, regulatory surprises)
  • Bird-in-Hand worksheet (Who am I? What do I know? Who do I know?)
  • Affordable Loss calculator

Timing & Phases

0–15 min · Frame the Scenario
Each team receives a plausible new-venture brief with a deliberate information gap (the uncertainty). Lecturer debriefs Knight, Furr and the VUCA frame.
15–35 min · Bird-in-Hand
Each team inventories means: identities, knowledge, networks. No external data allowed. What could you start today with what is already on the table?
35–55 min · Draw a Constraint Card
A black card is dealt: the market shifts, a regulator intervenes, a partner pulls out. Teams must respond using Affordable Loss and Lemonade principles.
55–75 min · Draw a Partnership Card
A new potential partner appears with a proposal. Apply Crazy Quilt: do you commit? On what terms? What do they change?
75–90 min · 24-Hour Plan
Each team produces a written 24-hour action plan — the first steps a Pilot-in-the-Plane would take. Pitch to neighbouring teams; receive one question.

Deliverable

Required output

A one-page 24-hour action plan per team, explicitly naming which effectuation principle governed each move.

MBA301 · Unit 3 · Activity

Strategy Canvas Lab

Strategy Design

© 2026 Dr. H. Haas · EU Business School

FormatStrategy Canvas Lab
Duration120 minutes
GroupingTeams of 4–5

Brief

Teams draft a Business Model Canvas, overlay a DVF check, and stress-test with Porter's Five Forces.

Learning Objectives

  • Translate a market opportunity into a coherent nine-block Business Model Canvas.
  • Triangulate desirability, viability and feasibility through the DVF overlay.
  • Stress-test the strategy against Porter's Five Forces and identify the most vulnerable block.

Materials

  • A1 BMC poster (one per team)
  • Coloured sticky notes
  • DVF overlay template
  • Porter's Five Forces worksheet
  • Red pens for stress-testing

Timing & Phases

0–15 min · Opportunity Brief
Each team receives a one-paragraph market opportunity (real firm, sanitised). Lecturer explains the BMC blocks and interdependencies.
15–55 min · BMC Build
Teams draft all nine blocks. Rule: use sticky notes so elements can be moved. No block is complete until it has been shown to inter-depend with at least two others.
55–80 min · DVF Overlay
Teams mark each block as D (desirable), V (viable), F (feasible) — or a combination. Blocks with only one letter are risks.
80–105 min · Porter's Five Forces Attack
Each team passes their BMC to a rival team, who plays Porter adversary and marks vulnerabilities in red. Original team defends and patches.
105–120 min · Board Pitch
3-minute team pitch focusing on the most robust block and the most fragile one — and the experiment they would run next.

Deliverable

Required output

A completed BMC with DVF annotations, a Five Forces risk register, and a single prioritised next-experiment brief per team.

Pitch Theatre

The Art of Pitching

© 2026 Dr. H. Haas · EU Business School

FormatPitch Theatre
Duration120 minutes
GroupingTeams of 3 (founder · co-founder · advisor roles)

Brief

Distill → Decompose → Prioritise, then a 3-minute live pitch to an investor panel.

Learning Objectives

  • Apply Bostelaar's Distill-Decompose-Prioritise protocol to reduce a muddy idea to a one-sentence core message.
  • Structure a 10-slide pitch deck using Kawasaki's and StoryBrand's architectures.
  • Deliver and absorb hostile investor questions with composure.

Materials

  • Bostelaar Core Message Worksheet (paragraph → 3 sentences → 1 sentence)
  • 10-slide deck template
  • Investor Q card deck (20 hostile questions)
  • Pitch rubric

Timing & Phases

0–20 min · Distill Your Core Message
Each team writes the long paragraph describing their venture. Then three sentences. Then one. If you cannot reduce it to one, you do not have a pitch — you have a draft.
20–45 min · Decompose & Prioritise
List every concept embedded in the paragraph as an isolated note. Force-rank from most to least important. What is the one claim that, if believed, makes everything else follow?
45–80 min · Build the 10-Slide Deck
Using Kawasaki's architecture (Problem · Solution · Model · Underlying Magic · Marketing · Competition · Team · Projections · Status · Ask). Overlay the StoryBrand arc: a character · with a problem · meets a guide · who gives a plan · and calls them to action.
80–100 min · Rehearsal & Q Prep
Each team rehearses the 3-minute pitch once. Then draws five investor Q cards and drafts responses under 30 seconds each.
100–120 min · Pitch Theatre
Live pitch to the panel (instructor + two peer teams). 3 minutes of pitch, 3 minutes of Q, 1 minute of panel verdict.

Deliverable

Required output

A 10-slide deck with one-sentence core message on the opening slide, plus recorded video of the live pitch for self-review.

MBA301 · Unit 5 · Activity

Opportunity Scan

Developing Successful Business Ideas

© 2026 Dr. H. Haas · EU Business School

FormatOpportunity Scan
Duration120 minutes (+ 30 min field prep the week before)
GroupingTeams of 3–4

Brief

JTBD interviews → Problem–Solution Fit → three testable experiments.

Learning Objectives

  • Run a Jobs-to-Be-Done interview without leading the interviewee.
  • Translate raw JTBD data into a Problem–Solution Fit hypothesis.
  • Design three riskiest-assumption experiments using the Javelin Experiment Board.

Materials

  • JTBD interview guide (5 open questions)
  • Lean Canvas A3 template
  • Javelin Experiment Board
  • Problem–Solution Fit worksheet
  • Timer

Timing & Phases

Pre-class · Field Interviews
Each team conducts 3 JTBD interviews with real customers in a chosen category. Raw audio or verbatim notes required.
0–20 min · Synthesis
Teams cluster quotes into jobs (functional, emotional, social). Identify the most frequently mentioned job.
20–50 min · Lean Canvas Draft
Fill problem, customer segment, unique value proposition, solution. Leave unfair advantage blank — it rarely exists at this stage, and that's fine.
50–75 min · Problem–Solution Fit Test
Articulate the riskiest assumption. Name the evidence that would falsify it.
75–110 min · Experiment Design
Build three experiments on the Javelin Board: hypothesis · method · success criteria. Each must be runnable in under a week.
110–120 min · Peer Review
Rotate boards. Each team reviews another's and marks the weakest experiment. Original team revises.

Deliverable

Required output

One Lean Canvas + three experiment briefs per team, ready to field-test in the following week.

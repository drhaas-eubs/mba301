MBA301 · Unit 6 · Activity

Foundry Simulation

Moving from an Idea to an Entrepreneurial Firm

© 2026 Dr. H. Haas · EU Business School

FormatFoundry Simulation
Duration120 minutes
GroupingTeams of 4 (assigned founder roles: CEO, CTO, CMO, COO)

Brief

Stand up a fictional firm — legal form, cap table, runway, and first hire.

Learning Objectives

  • Make the key legal, financial and team decisions every new venture must make in its first 90 days.
  • Build a founders' cap table and defend it in negotiation.
  • Calculate runway, burn rate and a rational first-hire sequence.

Materials

  • Founder role cards
  • Legal Forms Matrix worksheet
  • Founder's Pie equity calculator (Demmler)
  • Term sheet mini-glossary
  • Runway spreadsheet template

Timing & Phases

0–15 min · Charter
Team drafts a one-sentence company charter, sets equity expectations, and picks a legal form (UG · GmbH · Ltd · Delaware C-Corp) with rationale.
15–45 min · Cap Table Build
Using Demmler's Founder's Pie method, teams allocate equity across founders based on idea, commitment, risk, responsibility and expertise. Negotiate. Document.
45–70 min · Runway Math
Teams are given a fictional €250k seed. Calculate burn at different team sizes, project runway in months, and decide what milestone the runway must reach to justify a Series A.
70–100 min · First Hire Decision
Teams must choose: a senior engineer, a junior product marketer, a part-time CFO, or no hire. Defend the choice against runway and milestones.
100–120 min · Investor Pitch
3-minute pitch to neighbouring teams playing angel investors. One-page term sheet response required.

Deliverable

Required output

Charter + cap table + 18-month runway model + first-hire decision memo, all documented on a single A3 founder's pack.

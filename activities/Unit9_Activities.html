MBA301 · Unit 9 · Activity

Systems Mapping Lab · Capstone

Systems Thinking

© 2026 Dr. H. Haas · EU Business School

FormatSystems Mapping Lab · Capstone
Duration180 minutes (final capstone session)
GroupingTeams of 4–5

Brief

A causal loop diagram of a wicked problem + a leverage-point intervention pitch.

Learning Objectives

  • Build a causal loop diagram of a real wicked problem, including at least two feedback loops.
  • Locate the system's existing leverage points using Meadows' hierarchy.
  • Design one intervention at a high-leverage point and defend it against unintended consequences.

Materials

  • Large format paper (A1)
  • Fine markers in three colours
  • Meadows' 12 Leverage Points reference card
  • Systems Archetype library
  • Intervention brief template

Timing & Phases

0–20 min · Problem Selection
Each team picks a wicked problem from a curated list (fast fashion supply chains, urban traffic, platform moderation, antibiotic resistance, university rankings). Rittel & Webber's wicked-problem criteria must be demonstrated.
20–50 min · Iceberg Analysis
Teams work through the four levels: visible events → patterns over time → underlying structures → mental models. The mental-models layer is mandatory — most system maps fail because they stop at structures.
50–90 min · Causal Loop Diagram
Teams build the CLD: variables as nouns, arrows with polarity, loops labelled R (reinforcing) or B (balancing). At least one systems archetype must be identified.
90–120 min · Leverage Point Analysis
Against Meadows' 12, teams identify the three highest-leverage points available in their system. Which is closest to paradigm?
120–160 min · Intervention Design
Design one concrete intervention at a high-leverage point. Write its brief, its measurable success criteria, and its two most likely unintended consequences.
160–180 min · Capstone Pitch
5-minute final capstone pitch to the full class. Each team presents diagram, leverage analysis, and intervention. Peer and instructor assessment against the course rubric.

Deliverable

Required output

A1 causal loop diagram + leverage-point assessment + intervention brief + 5-minute capstone pitch. This is the graded capstone artefact for the course.

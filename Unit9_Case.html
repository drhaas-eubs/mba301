UNIT 9 · CASE STUDY

Fashion's Circular Future

Industry-scale wicked problem · 2010–2040Systems Thinking

Background

The global fashion industry produces between 8% and 10% of global CO₂ emissions, uses 93 billion cubic metres of water annually, and has accelerated from 2 seasonal collections per year (1990) to 24+ micro-drops per year (2020s). Textile waste has risen 40% in the last decade. The case sits at the intersection of consumer behaviour, supply-chain structure, regulatory frameworks, and technology — a canonical wicked problem suited to systems-thinking tools.

Key Facts

  • Emissions: 8–10% of global CO₂ — more than aviation + shipping combined
  • Water use: 93 billion m³/yr (cotton + textile dyeing)
  • Pace: 2 collections/yr (1990) → 24+ micro-drops/yr (today)
  • Waste: 92 million tonnes textile waste annually
  • Regulation: EU Strategy for Sustainable and Circular Textiles (2022)
  • Stakeholders: consumers · brands · suppliers · workers · regulators · NGOs

Discussion Questions

  1. Fill the Iceberg for the fast-fashion system. What events, patterns, structures, and mental models would you put at each level?
  2. Draw a causal loop diagram with at least 2 reinforcing and 2 balancing loops. Where are the delays?
  3. Which of Meadows' 12 leverage points is most ignored by current industry initiatives (e.g., recycling programmes)?
  4. Design one intervention at one of Meadows' top-3 leverage points. Describe who must sense, presence, and realise the change (Theory U).

References

  • Meadows, D. (1999). Leverage Points — Places to Intervene in a System.
  • Ellen MacArthur Foundation (2017). A New Textiles Economy.

UNIT 6 · PRE-READING

Strategies for Learning from Failure

Amy C. EdmondsonHarvard Business Review · 2011
Key framework. A Spectrum of Reasons for Failure

Key Takeaways

  1. Not all failures are equal — the spectrum runs from blameworthy (deviance, inattention) to praiseworthy (thoughtful experimentation).
  2. Most organisations conflate the two — punishing intelligent failure while tolerating process deviation.
  3. Psychological safety is the single most predictive variable of whether an organisation learns from its failures.
  4. Post-mortems fail when they ask 'who?' before they ask 'why?'
  5. Intelligent failure has pre-declared learning goals — without them, it's just failure.

Discussion Questions

  1. Where on the failure spectrum does your organisation's last significant failure sit? How was it handled?
  2. What would it take to run an 'intelligent failure' programme explicitly inside your team?
  3. Why are pre-mortems more reliable than post-mortems for learning?
Edmondson, A.C. (2011). Strategies for Learning from Failure. Harvard Business Review, 89(4), 48–55.

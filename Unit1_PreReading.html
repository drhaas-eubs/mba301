Design Thinking

Tim BrownHarvard Business Review · 2008
Key framework. Desirability × Feasibility × Viability (DVF)

Key Takeaways

  1. Design thinking starts with users, not with business problems.
  2. Prototyping is a form of thinking out loud — build to think, not the reverse.
  3. Fuzzy front-ends are where insight is earned; structured back-ends are where it's delivered.
  4. DVF overlap is the sweet spot — miss any of the three and you have an engineering project, a charity, or a fantasy.
  5. Empathy scales only when designers stay physically close to users.

Discussion Questions

  1. Which of the three DVF lenses is hardest to hold in tension at your organisation?
  2. Recall a project where the desirability dimension was neglected. What happened?
  3. How would you introduce DVF to a team that has only worked in feasibility?
Brown, T. (2008). Design Thinking. Harvard Business Review, 86(6), 84–92.

UNIT 6 · CASE STUDY

Sentient — Funding to Dissolution

Capital structure lens · 2007–2019Moving from an Idea to an Entrepreneurial Firm

Background

Unit 5 read Sentient through the JTBD lens. This unit reads the same venture through capital structure. Sentient raised $143.5M across five rounds from top-tier investors including Tata Communications, Access Industries, and Horizons Ventures — yet the company was unable to produce a cash-generating business and wound down in 2019. The case illuminates funding-ladder decisions that looked brilliant in isolation but misaligned with the underlying unit economics.

Key Facts

  • Round 1 (2008): $23M Series A — Tata Communications
  • Round 2 (2011): $50M Series B
  • Round 3 (2014): $103.5M Series C — largest AI Series C to date
  • Round 4–5: extensions and strategic capital
  • Burn: estimated $10–15M/year at peak (large compute infrastructure)
  • Revenue: never disclosed publicly; reportedly below $10M/yr in peak years
  • Dissolution (2019): core tech sold to Evolv and Cognizant

Discussion Questions

  1. Compute a plausible CAC and LTV for Sentient Ascend in 2017 given a 6-month enterprise sales cycle and 2-year contracts.
  2. What LTV/CAC ratio would the company have needed to justify the cumulative $143.5M in capital?
  3. At which round should the investors have imposed tougher kill criteria? What metrics would you have demanded?
  4. Compare Sentient's cap table to a capital-efficient alternative. How many rounds could the company have skipped?

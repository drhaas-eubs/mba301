UNIT 7 · CASE STUDY

Skydeck — DB Systel

10-week corporate accelerator · 2020–presentIntrapreneurship

Background

Skydeck is the internal accelerator programme of DB Systel — the IT subsidiary of Deutsche Bahn. Launched in 2020, each cohort runs a 10-week programme where 5 multidisciplinary teams work with 12 internal client projects. Skydeck is the best-documented European case of a structured corporate accelerator that balances parent-organisation strategic alignment with startup-style experimentation rhythms.

Key Facts

  • Parent: DB Systel · IT division of Deutsche Bahn · 4,400+ employees
  • Format: 10 weeks · 5 teams · 12 internal client projects per cohort
  • Gates: Discovery (wk 3) · Validation (wk 6) · Traction (wk 9) · Demo Day (wk 10)
  • Team size: 3–4 people per venture team · full-time allocation
  • Sponsor model: each team has a senior DB sponsor + an engineer-founder
  • Outcomes: projects either transition to DB Systel, spin out, or close

Discussion Questions

  1. Which of the 5 corporate venture models (incubator / accelerator / studio / CVC / spin-out) does Skydeck best exemplify? Which features are hybrid?
  2. What role does the sponsor-engineer model play in protecting teams from corporate antibodies?
  3. Propose 3 kill criteria that a Skydeck-style gate process must include. Defend why each is objective rather than political.
  4. How would you adapt Skydeck for a parent organisation with no internal engineering talent?

UNIT 3 · PRE-READING

Design for Action

Tim Brown & Roger L. MartinHarvard Business Review · 2015
Key framework. The 10 Practices of Strategy Design

Key Takeaways

  1. Strategic plans fail not because they're wrong but because they're not designed to be acted on.
  2. Treating strategy as a design problem — DVF, prototyping, iteration — increases implementation rates dramatically.
  3. The 10 practices fall into three clusters: enable the user (2), build the prototype (4), design the handoff (4).
  4. The single most-neglected practice is 'prototype the plan' — most firms prototype the product but not the plan for deploying it.
  5. Strategy design and product design must be staffed by the same iterative culture.

Discussion Questions

  1. Which of the 10 practices does your organisation do worst? What's the cost?
  2. Identify one strategic initiative where 'prototype the plan' would have changed the outcome.
  3. How would the BMC change if each of the 9 blocks had to be prototyped separately?
Brown, T., & Martin, R.L. (2015). Design for Action. Harvard Business Review, 93(9), 56–64.

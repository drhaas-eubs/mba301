UNIT 4 · ACTIVITY

Uncertainty Challenge

90 min · Teams of 3Entrepreneurial Thinking

Objective

Apply all 5 effectuation principles to a deliberately underspecified brief in which the goal is ambiguous and the resources must be self-identified.

Materials

  • Brief card (provided)
  • Bird-in-Hand worksheet
  • Affordable Loss calculator
  • Possibility Quadrant handout
  • Pre-mortem template

Steps

Step 1 · 5 min
Read the Brief

Brief: 'Create something of value to at least one real person by the end of the session, using only what is in this room.' That is the entire brief.

Step 2 · 15 min · Team
Bird-in-Hand Inventory

Individually write: Who am I? What do I know? Whom do I know? Pool answers. You may not add resources from outside the room.

Step 3 · 10 min
Affordable Loss

Each team commits: how much time, money, reputation can we afford to lose on this? That's your upper bound.

Step 4 · 10 min
Possibility Quadrant

Place the brief on the 2×2 — certainty of cause × certainty of outcome. Which quadrant are you in? Which tools does it call for?

Step 5 · 15 min
Pre-Mortem

Imagine it's 5pm and the project has failed catastrophically. Work backwards: what went wrong? List 5 failure modes now.

Step 6 · 25 min
Execute

Using only Bird-in-Hand resources and respecting Affordable Loss, produce something of value. Pivot freely (Lemonade). Form partnerships across teams (Crazy Quilt).

Step 7 · 10 min · Plenary
Demo

Each team shows what they made and names: the surprise that reshaped direction; the partnership that emerged; the assumption that didn't hold.

Deliverable. Artifact photo + 200-word effectuation reflection naming which of the 5 principles dominated the session.

Rubric

Knowledge & Understanding 20% · Application 30% · Critical Thinking 25% · Communication 15% · AI Use 10%.

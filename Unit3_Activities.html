UNIT 3 · ACTIVITY

Strategy Canvas Lab

2 × 90 min sessionsStrategy Design

Objective

Build a complete strategic picture of one real venture using BMC, Porter's 5F, and Blue Ocean's Strategy Canvas — then stress-test it with the DVF assessment.

Materials

  • BMC canvas (A1)
  • Porter 5F worksheet
  • Strategy Canvas template
  • Bostelaar DVF/BMC evidence sheet
  • Sticky notes

Steps

Step 1 · 10 min
Select a Venture

Pick one real venture (yours, an employer, or a public case). Name it, locate it in an industry.

Step 2 · 25 min · Team
BMC Draft

Complete all 9 BMC blocks. Don't polish; get a v1 on the canvas so you can debate it.

Step 3 · 20 min
Porter's 5 Forces Stress-Test

Score each of the 5 forces (1=weak threat, 5=strong). Identify the two most dangerous forces.

Step 4 · 25 min
Strategy Canvas

Pick 6–8 competitive factors for your industry. Plot your curve against the industry standard. Identify the ERRC moves.

Step 5 · 25 min
DVF Evidence Assessment

Apply Bostelaar's evidence framework. For each BMC block, rate evidence quality for Desirability, Viability, Feasibility (strong / moderate / weak / none). Mark blocks where evidence is weakest.

Step 6 · 25 min
Blue Ocean Pivot

Based on the weakest DVF blocks + Porter's strongest forces, propose one Blue Ocean move using the 4 Actions framework.

Step 7 · 20 min
Strategy Pitch

Each team pitches: 'Here's our BMC. Here's our ERRC move. Here's why it survives Porter.'

Deliverable. Digital BMC + annotated Strategy Canvas + 500-word narrative explaining the proposed Blue Ocean move. PDF upload.

Rubric

Knowledge & Understanding 20% · Application 30% · Critical Thinking 25% · Communication 15% · AI Use 10%.
